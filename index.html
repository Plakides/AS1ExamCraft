How to self-mark

Award marks the way an examiner would

  1. Read the mark points carefully. Each point is one specific idea an examiner is looking for.
  2. In your own answer, drag-select the phrase or sentence that you think shows that idea.
  3. Click the matching mark point. Your highlighted text will turn that mark point's colour.
  4. Be honest. Award the mark only if your phrasing is clear enough that an examiner would credit it. Vague wording or missing keywords usually means no mark.
  5. To remove a mark, click the highlight in your answer or use the "Remove" button on the awarded mark point card.
  6. When finished, click "Finish Marking" for examiner-style feedback and a model answer.
